SEBI CIRCULAR ON ALGORITHMIC TRADING BY RETAIL INVESTORS
=========================================================

CIRCULAR NO: SEBI/HO/MRD/TPD-1/P/CIR/2025/027
DATE: February 4, 2025
SUBJECT: Framework for Algorithmic Trading by Retail Investors
APPLICABLE TO: Stock Exchanges, Clearing Corporations, Depositories,
               Stock Brokers, and Algo Providers

This circular establishes the comprehensive regulatory framework for
algorithmic trading by retail investors in India. It supersedes all
prior interim guidelines and circulars on this subject.

The framework aims to balance innovation in retail algo trading with
investor protection, market integrity, and systemic risk management.

SECTION 1: ORDER FREQUENCY THRESHOLDS
─────────────────────────────────────

1.1 CLASSIFICATION OF RETAIL ALGORITHMIC TRADERS

Retail investors deploying algorithmic trading strategies through
broker-provided APIs shall be classified into the following categories
based on their order frequency:

(a) TECH-SAVVY RETAIL INVESTORS:
    - Maximum order frequency: Less than 10 orders per second
    - Registration requirement: Light registration through broker
    - The broker must maintain a registry of all tech-savvy retail
      clients using algorithmic order placement
    - No separate exchange-level registration needed for the investor
    - The broker bears responsibility for monitoring compliance

(b) HIGH-FREQUENCY RETAIL ALGO TRADERS:
    - Order frequency: 10 or more orders per second
    - Registration requirement: Full exchange algo registration
    - Must submit strategy documentation to the exchange
    - Subject to enhanced surveillance and monitoring
    - Must maintain collateral deposits as prescribed by exchange

1.2 ORDER-TO-TRADE RATIO MONITORING

Exchanges shall implement real-time monitoring of the order-to-trade
ratio (OTR) for all algorithmic orders. The prescribed limits are:

    - Equity cash segment: OTR not to exceed 50:1
    - Equity derivatives segment: OTR not to exceed 100:1
    - Currency derivatives segment: OTR not to exceed 100:1

Algos consistently breaching OTR limits may be suspended by the
exchange without prior notice, and the broker must be informed
immediately.

1.3 THROTTLING AND RATE LIMITING

Exchanges must implement per-client throttling mechanisms that:
    - Track order count per second at the client level
    - Automatically reject orders that would breach the 10/sec limit
    - Send real-time alerts to the broker when a client approaches
      80% of the threshold (8 orders/sec sustained over 5 seconds)
    - Maintain a 30-day rolling log of all throttling events

For investment-style algorithms with holding periods exceeding one
trading day, the effective order frequency is typically well below
1 order per second and does not trigger frequency limits.

SECTION 2: BLACK-BOX vs TRANSPARENT ALGORITHM CLASSIFICATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1 DEFINITIONS

(a) TRANSPARENT (WHITE-BOX) ALGORITHM:
    An algorithm whose trading logic can be fully explained in clear,
    auditable terms to the broker, exchange, or regulator. The entry
    and exit conditions must be expressible as deterministic rules
    that a qualified compliance officer can independently verify.

    Examples of white-box strategies:
    - Moving average crossover (e.g., buy when 50-day SMA crosses
      above 200-day SMA)
    - RSI-based mean reversion (e.g., buy when RSI < 30, sell > 70)
    - Price-volume breakout (e.g., buy when price breaks 52-week high
      with 2x average volume)
    - Bollinger Band mean reversion

(b) BLACK-BOX ALGORITHM:
    An algorithm whose trading logic cannot be explained in clear,
    auditable terms. This classification applies when:

    (i)   The entry/exit logic is determined by a machine learning
          model, neural network, deep learning system, or large
          language model (LLM) without explicit rule documentation.

    (ii)  The signal source (e.g., sentiment derived from news,
          social media, or alternative data) cannot be independently
          verified, replicated, or audited.

    (iii) The logic description provided to the broker is insufficient
          for a qualified compliance officer to understand the specific
          conditions under which trades are placed.

    (iv)  The algorithm adapts its parameters dynamically based on
          market conditions without a documented adaptation framework.

2.2 REQUIREMENTS FOR BLACK-BOX ALGORITHMS

If a strategy is classified as black-box, the algo provider must:

    (a) Register as a Research Analyst (RA) with SEBI under the
        SEBI (Research Analysts) Regulations, 2014.

    (b) Maintain detailed records of model training data, feature
        selection methodology, and model validation results.

    (c) Provide quarterly model performance reports to the broker.

    (d) Implement model risk management controls including:
        - Out-of-sample testing documentation
        - Model degradation monitoring
        - Fallback rules when model confidence is low

2.3 REQUIREMENTS FOR WHITE-BOX ALGORITHMS

White-box strategies must provide:
    - Explicit entry/exit conditions in natural language AND code
    - Mathematical or rule-based signal definitions
    - Complete logic description auditable by non-technical reviewers
    - Source attribution for all data inputs (price, volume, fundamentals)
    - Backtested performance with clear methodology documentation

SECTION 3: MANDATORY EXCHANGE-ASSIGNED ALGO TAG IDS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1 ALGO TAG ID FORMAT AND ASSIGNMENT

Every algorithmic trading strategy deployed by a retail investor must
receive a unique Algo Tag ID from the relevant stock exchange. The
format shall be:

    NSE-ALGO-{YEAR}-{UNIQUE_ID}

Where:
    - NSE/BSE: The exchange code
    - ALGO: Fixed identifier for algorithmic strategies
    - YEAR: Four-digit year of registration
    - UNIQUE_ID: Eight-character alphanumeric identifier assigned by
      the exchange registration system

Example: NSE-ALGO-2025-A3F7B2C1

3.2 TAGGING REQUIREMENTS

All orders generated by a registered algorithm must include:

    (a) The Algo Tag ID in every order submitted to the exchange
    (b) A unique order correlation identifier linking the order to
        the specific signal that generated it
    (c) Timestamp of signal generation (when the algo decided to trade)
    (d) Timestamp of order submission (when the order reached the
        exchange matching engine)

3.3 RE-REGISTRATION

    (a) Algo Tag IDs are valid for one calendar year and must be
        renewed annually through the broker.
    (b) Any material modification to the strategy logic, parameters,
        or risk controls requires re-registration and a new Algo Tag ID.
    (c) Minor parameter adjustments (e.g., stop-loss percentage changes
        within a pre-approved range) do not require re-registration
        but must be documented.

SECTION 4: BROKER RESPONSIBILITY FRAMEWORK
──────────────────────────────────────────

4.1 BROKER AS PRINCIPAL

Under the SEBI framework, the stock broker is the "principal" in all
algorithmic trading activities conducted through its platform:

    (a) The broker bears PRIMARY responsibility for all orders placed
        through its systems, including algorithmic orders placed by
        retail investors using the broker's API.

    (b) The broker must implement the following controls:

        PRE-TRADE RISK CONTROLS:
        - Maximum order value limits per client per day
        - Maximum position limits per client (single stock and aggregate)
        - Price band checks (reject orders beyond exchange circuit limits)
        - Order rate limiting per client (enforce 10/sec threshold)

        REAL-TIME MONITORING:
        - Continuous surveillance of all active algo strategies
        - Automatic alerts on unusual trading patterns
        - Dashboard visibility into per-client algo performance

        KILL-SWITCH CAPABILITY:
        - Ability to halt ANY algo immediately at broker level
        - Must be operable by risk management team 24x7 during market hours
        - Quarterly kill-switch testing mandatory

    (c) Daily risk exposure reports must be generated and archived.

4.2 ALGO PROVIDER AS AGENT

    (a) The algorithm provider (whether the retail investor or a
        third-party vendor) acts as the "agent."

    (b) The agent is responsible for:
        - Accuracy of strategy logic documentation
        - Timely notification of any strategy modifications
        - Cooperation with broker's risk management team
        - Reporting of any technical failures or anomalous behavior

    (c) Liability: While the broker cannot transfer regulatory
        liability to the agent, the broker may contractually seek
        indemnification from the algo provider/investor for losses
        arising from strategy malfunctions.

SECTION 5: RESEARCH ANALYST REGISTRATION FOR BLACK-BOX PROVIDERS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5.1 APPLICABILITY

Any entity or individual providing black-box algorithmic strategies
to retail investors (directly or through a broker platform) must
register as a Research Analyst under SEBI (RA) Regulations, 2014.

5.2 REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS

    (a) QUALIFICATION: NISM Series XV certification mandatory
    (b) NET WORTH: Minimum ₹5 lakh for individuals, ₹50 lakh for
        body corporates
    (c) INFRASTRUCTURE: Adequate compliance and IT infrastructure
    (d) COMPLIANCE OFFICER: Designated compliance officer required
        for body corporates

5.3 ONGOING OBLIGATIONS

Registered RA providing algo strategies must:

    (a) Disclose all material conflicts of interest
    (b) Maintain records of all research and recommendations for
        a minimum of 5 years
    (c) Not guarantee returns or provide misleading performance claims
    (d) Clearly label all algo outputs as "research" and not
        "investment advice" unless also registered as an Investment
        Adviser (IA)
    (e) Submit quarterly compliance reports to SEBI
    (f) Undergo annual SEBI inspection if requested

SECTION 6: INVESTOR DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS
───────────────────────────────────────────

6.1 MANDATORY DISCLOSURES

Brokers offering algorithmic trading to retail clients must provide
the following disclosures BEFORE enabling algo trading:

    (a) PAST PERFORMANCE DISCLOSURE:
        - Backtested performance with time period and methodology
        - Live paper trading performance (if available)
        - Prominent disclaimer: "Past performance (backtested or live)
          is not indicative of future results"
        - Maximum historical drawdown with date range

    (b) RISK FACTOR DISCLOSURE:
        - Algorithmic trading involves risks of technical failures
        - Market conditions may change, invalidating strategy logic
        - Slippage between backtested and live execution prices
        - Concentration risk from automated position building
        - System downtime risk and contingency procedures

    (c) FEE DISCLOSURE:
        - All charges related to API access, algo hosting, and
          data feeds must be clearly disclosed upfront
        - Performance-based fee structures must be separately approved

6.2 ONGOING REPORTING

    (a) Monthly performance statements to algo trading clients
    (b) Real-time position visibility through broker dashboard
    (c) Immediate notification of any strategy suspension or
        parameter change

SECTION 7: POST-TRADE AUDIT TRAIL REQUIREMENTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────

7.1 AUDIT TRAIL COMPONENTS

The broker must maintain a complete audit trail for every algo order
consisting of:

    (a) SIGNAL AUDIT:
        - Timestamp of signal generation
        - Market data snapshot at signal time
        - Strategy parameters active at signal time
        - Signal direction (buy/sell), confidence level, and rationale

    (b) ORDER AUDIT:
        - Order type (market, limit, stop-loss)
        - Order price and quantity
        - Timestamp of order submission to exchange
        - Exchange acknowledgement timestamp
        - Order status updates (open, partial fill, filled, cancelled)

    (c) EXECUTION AUDIT:
        - Fill price and quantity
        - Slippage from signal price
        - Market impact estimate
        - Settlement confirmation

    (d) RISK EVENT AUDIT:
        - Stop-loss trigger events
        - Take-profit trigger events
        - Position limit breach events
        - Kill-switch activation events
        - Any manual intervention by broker risk team

7.2 RETENTION POLICY

    (a) All audit trail records must be preserved for a minimum of
        7 (seven) years from the date of the trade.
    (b) Records must be maintained in a tamper-evident format.
    (c) Records must be searchable by: Algo Tag ID, date range,
        client ID, and ticker symbol.
    (d) SEBI may request access to audit trail records at any time
        during the retention period.

7.3 FORMAT REQUIREMENTS

    (a) Audit data must be stored in structured format (JSON or XML)
    (b) Each record must include the Algo Tag ID for traceability
    (c) Timestamps must be in ISO 8601 format with millisecond
        precision and IST timezone designation

SECTION 8: PAPER TRADING AND PRE-DEPLOYMENT REQUIREMENTS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

8.1 MANDATORY PAPER TRADING PERIOD

    (a) All new algorithmic strategies must undergo a minimum 30-day
        paper trading period before live deployment.
    (b) Paper trading must be conducted in real market conditions
        using live market data feeds.
    (c) The paper trading environment must simulate realistic
        execution conditions including:
        - Order book impact estimation
        - Realistic fill assumptions (not guaranteed fills)
        - Brokerage and exchange charges
        - Slippage estimation based on historical data

8.2 PAPER TRADING EVALUATION CRITERIA

Before approving live deployment, the broker must verify:

    (a) Strategy performance meets the stated backtest expectations
        within a reasonable tolerance (±30% of backtested returns)
    (b) Maximum drawdown during paper trading does not exceed the
        stated stop-loss parameters
    (c) No technical failures or erroneous orders during paper trading
    (d) Order frequency remains within the approved limits
    (e) The strategy completes at least 10 round-trip trades during
        the paper trading period

SECTION 9: POSITION SIZING AND PORTFOLIO CONCENTRATION LIMITS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.1 SINGLE POSITION LIMITS

    (a) No single algorithmic strategy shall deploy more than 10%
        of the total portfolio value in a single position.
    (b) Positions exceeding 10% but not exceeding 20% shall be
        flagged and require explicit acknowledgement from the investor.
    (c) Positions exceeding 20% of portfolio value shall be
        automatically rejected by the broker's risk management system.

9.2 AGGREGATE PORTFOLIO LIMITS

    (a) Total capital deployed across ALL algorithmic strategies
        shall not exceed 15% of the total portfolio value without
        explicit risk committee approval.
    (b) Correlation-adjusted exposure: If two or more strategies
        trade highly correlated instruments (correlation > 0.7),
        their combined exposure counts as a single concentration risk.
    (c) Sector concentration: No more than 25% of algo-deployed
        capital in a single sector.

SECTION 10: GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
──────────────────────────────

10.1 A separate grievance redressal mechanism for algorithmic trading
     complaints must be established by every broker offering algo
     trading services to retail clients.

10.2 Response timeline:
     - Acknowledgement: Within 24 hours of complaint receipt
     - Resolution: Within 7 business days
     - Escalation to exchange: If unresolved within 15 business days

10.3 Brokers must maintain a dedicated algo trading helpdesk with
     staff trained in algorithmic trading concepts and the SEBI
     regulatory framework.
